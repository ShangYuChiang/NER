Molecular diversity of PBAN Gene family peptides from fire ants. The PBAN Gene / Pyrokinin Gene peptide family is a major neuropeptide family characterized with a common FXPRLamide in the C-termini. These peptides are ubiquitously distributed in the Insecta and are involved in many essential endocrinal functions, e.g., pheromone production. Previous work demonstrated the localization of PBAN Gene in the fire ant central nervous system, and identified a new family of PBAN Gene from the red imported fire ant, Solenopsis invicta. In this study, we identified five more PBAN Gene / Pyrokinin Gene genes from S. geminata, S. richteri, S. pergandii, S. carolinensis, and a hybrid of S. invicta and S. richteri. The gene sequences were used to determine the phylogenetic relationships of these species and hybrid, which compared well to the morphologically defined fire ant subgroup complexes. The putative PBAN Gene and other peptides were determined from the amino acid sequences of the PBAN Gene / pyrokinin Gene genes. We summarized all known insect PBAN Gene family neuropeptides, and for the first time constructed a phylogenetic tree based on the full amino acid sequences translated from representative PBAN Gene cDNAs. The PBAN Gene / pyrokinin Gene gene is well conserved in Insecta and probably extends into the Arthropod phylum; however, translated pre-propeptides may vary and functional diversity may be retained, lost, or modified during the evolutionary process.
