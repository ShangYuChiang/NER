Transcription factor E93 Gene regulates wing development by directly promoting Dpp Gene signaling in Drosophila. Transcription factor E93 Gene is a steroid hormone ecdysone early response gene and plays crucial roles in both the degradation of larval tissues and the formation of adult organs during insect metamorphosis with the prepupal-pupal-adult transition. However, the molecular mechanism underlying E93 Gene regulation is poorly understood. In this study, we found that specific knockdown of the E93 Gene gene in the Drosophila wing disrupted wing development. Analyzing ChIP-seq signals for E93 Gene in Drosophila wing identified that the decapentaplegic Gene ( Dpp Gene ) gene was a potential downstream target of E93 Gene . ChIP-PCR analysis and dual-luciferase reporter assay confirmed that E93 Gene could bind to the Dpp Gene promoter and enhanced its activity. Furthermore, the expressions of Dpp Gene and other components in the Dpp Gene signaling pathway were upregulated following E93 Gene overexpression in Drosophila S2 cells but were decreased after E93 Gene knockdown in the wing. Moreover, the impairment of the Dpp Gene signaling pathway phenocopied the defects of E93 Gene knockdown on wing development. Taken together, our results suggest that E93 Gene modulates the Dpp Gene signaling pathway to regulate wing development during Drosophila metamorphosis.
