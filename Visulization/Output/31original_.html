Identification and RNAi-based function analysis of chitinase Gene family genes in diamondback moth, Plutella xylostella. BACKGROUND: Insect chitinases play a vital part in chitin degradation in exoskeletons and gut linings during the molting process, therefore are considered as the potential targets for new insecticide design or RNA interference (RNAi) based pest management. The systematic function analysis of chitinase Gene genes have already been well conducted in several insect pests, but still absent in Plutella xylostella. RESULTS: In the present study, 13 full-length chitinase Gene transcripts were obtained in P. xylostella. Developmental and tissue-specific expression pattern analysis revealed that seven chitinase Gene transcripts were periodically expressed during molting stage and mainly expressed in the integument or midgut, including PxCht3 Gene , PxCht5 Gene , PxCht6-2 Gene , PxCht7 Gene , PxCht8 Gene , PxCht10 Gene and PxCht-h. RNAi-mediated knockdown of these specific expressed genes revealed that PxCht5 Gene and PxCht10 were essential in larval molting, pupation and eclosion, and PxCht7 Gene was indispensable only in eclosion; No significant effects were observed on insect survival or normal development when the rest chitinase Gene transcripts were suppressed by RNAi. CONCLUSION: Our results indicated the function of P. xylostella chitinase Gene family genes during the molting process, and may provide potential targets for RNAi-based management of P. xylostella.
