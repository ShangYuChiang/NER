UDP-Glycosyltransferases Gene are involved in imidacloprid resistance in the Asian citrus psyllid, Diaphorina citri (Hemiptera: Lividae). UDP-glycosyltransferases Gene ( UGTs Gene ), as phase II detoxification enzymes, are widely distributed within living organisms and play vital roles in the biotransformation of endobiotics and xenobiotics in insects. Insects increase the expression of detoxification enzymes to cope with the stress of xenobiotics, including insecticides. However, the roles of UGTs Gene in insecticide resistance are still seldom reported. In this study, two UGT Gene inhibitors, namely, 5-nitrouracil Gene and sulfinpyrazone, were found to synergistically increase the toxicity of imidacloprid in the resistant population of Diaphorina citri. Based on transcriptome data, a total of 17 putative UGTs Gene were identified. Quantitative real-time PCR showed that fourteen of the 17 UGT Gene genes were overexpressed in the resistant population relative to the susceptible population. Using RNA interference technology to knockdown six UGT Gene genes, the results suggested that silencing the selected UGT375A1 Gene , UGT383A1 Gene , UGT383B1 Gene , and UGT384A1 Gene genes dramatically increased the toxicity of imidacloprid in the resistant population. However, silencing the UGT362B1 Gene and UGT379A1 Gene genes did not result in a significant increase in the toxicity of imidacloprid in the resistant population. These findings revealed that some upregulated UGT Gene genes were involved in imidacloprid resistance in D. citri. These results shed some light upon and further our understanding of the mechanisms of insecticide resistance in insects.
