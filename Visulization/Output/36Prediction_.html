Identification, expression and function of myosin heavy chain family genes in Tribolium castaneum. Functions of myosin heavy chain ( myosin Gene ) family genes are poorly understood in some insects. To address this, we determined the expression and function of myosin Gene family genes in Tribolium castaneum. TcMyo15 Gene is predominantly expressed in early embryos, late larvae and early adults, but TcMyo7B Gene transcripts significantly increased in late larvae. TcMyo20 Gene transcripts are abundant in pre-adults, whereas TcMhc1 Gene is highly expressed in post-embryonic stages. TcMhc2 Gene shows peak expression in late pupae. TcMyo9 Gene transcripts reached their highest levels in late pupae. TcMyo15 Gene , TcMyo7B Gene and TcMhc2 Gene are abundantly expressed in the adult epidermis, gut and testis, respectively. TcMyo9 Gene and TcMyo20 Gene are highly expressed in the epidermis, fat body and ovary, and TcMhc1 Gene exhibits high mRNA levels in the epidermis and accessory gland. TcMyo20 Gene RNAi reduced wing and leg size, fertility and egg hatchability. TcMyo9 Gene knockdown completely inhibited eclosion and fecundity, but TcMyo15 Gene or TcMhc1 Gene silencing only impaired eclosion. TcMhc2 Gene RNAi affected pupation and wing development. This study suggests myosin Gene genes as potential targets of pesticides due to their critical roles in insect development and reproduction.
