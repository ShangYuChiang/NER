CNAct-1 Gene gene is differentially expressed in the subtropical mosquito Culex nigripalpus (Diptera: Culicidae), the primary West Nile Virus vector in Florida. Analysis of differentially expressed genes is a common molecular biological tool to investigate changes in mosquito genes after a bloodmeal or parasite exposure. We report here the characterization of a differentially expressed actin Gene gene, CNAct-1 Gene , from the subtropical mosquito, Culex nigripalpus Theobald (Diptera: Culicidae). The CNAct-1 Gene genomic clone is 1.525 kb, includes one 66-bp intron, and a 328-bp 3 -untranslated region. The 376-amino acid putative translation product shares high similarity with muscle-specific actin Gene proteins from other insects, including Culex pipiens pipiens L., Aedes aegypti (L.), Anopheles gambiae Giles and Drosophila melanogaster (Meigen). CNAct-1 Gene is expressed in second and third instars, late pupae, and adult females and males. Interestingly, Cx. nigripalpus actin Gene was highly expressed in female mosquito midgut tissue isolated 6-12 h after ingestion of a bloodmeal. This expression profile indicates a unique function for CNAct-1 Gene in midgut processes that are initiated after blood ingestion.
