Bmo-miR-79 Gene downregulates the expression of BmEm4 Gene in the silkworm, Bombyx mori. MicroRNA is an important regulation factor in insect development and metamorphosis. It has been reported that E(spl)m4 Gene is a miRNA-targeted gene, as well as the target of the Notch signaling pathway in Drosophila. The expression of E(spl)m4 Gene can be regulated by microRNA and further affect the neural development of Drosophila. Here, we found that BmEm4 Gene , an ortholog of E(spl)m4 Gene from Bombyx mori, was the target gene of bmo-miR-79 Gene , with target sites containing the Brd and K boxes of the BmEm4_3 Gene UTR, which was validated by the dual luciferase reporter (DLR) assay. Furthermore, bmo-miR-79 Gene mimics can inhibit the expression of BmEm4 Gene in BmN cells after transfection, and bmo-miR-79 Gene can also inhibit the expression of BmEm4 Gene in different developmental stages of Bombyx mori at a posttranscriptional level, to different degrees. The EMSA test further showed that bmo-miR-79 Gene could bind to BmAGO2 Gene , which is the Bombyx mori argonaute2 Gene protein, suggesting that bmo-miR-79 Gene might regulate the expression of BmEm4 Gene by forming miRISC complexes with BmAGO2 Gene . Taken together, bmo-miR-79 Gene could regulate the expression of BmEm4 Gene mediated by BmAGO2 Gene and further affect its function in the silkworm Bombyx mori.
