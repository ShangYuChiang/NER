Life-span phenotypes of elav Gene and Rbp9 Gene in Drosophila suggest functional cooperation of the two ELAV Gene -family protein genes. The ELAV Gene family of RNA-binding proteins is involved in various aspects of the post-transcriptional regulation of gene expression, from alternative splicing to translation. The members of this family have been shown to interact with each other and have been suggested to function as homo- and/or hetero-multimers. However, the functional interactions among them have not been demonstrated in vivo. In this study, we examined the genetic interaction between elav Gene and Rbp9 Gene , two of the three genes encoding ELAV Gene -family proteins in Drosophila. Mutants of both elav Gene and Rbp9 Gene showed shorter life spans than the control, with elav Gene showing a shorter life span than Rbp9 Gene . The survival curve of elav Gene - Rbp9 Gene double-mutant flies was indistinguishable from that of elav Gene single-mutant flies, suggesting that both mutations affect longevity through the same pathway. Considering the fact that both genes are co-expressed in adult neurons, we hypothesize that ELAV Gene and Rbp9 Gene cooperate to maintain the functional integrity of the adult nervous system.
