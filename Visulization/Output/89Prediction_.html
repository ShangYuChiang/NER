BmCAP, a silkmoth gene encoding multiple protein isoforms characterized by SoHo and SH3 Gene domains: expression analysis during ovarian follicular development. CAP/ ArgBP2 Gene /vinexin family proteins, adaptor proteins characterized by three SH3 Gene domains at their C-termini and a SoHo domain towards their N-termini, are known to regulate cell adhesion, cytoskeletal organization, and growth factor signaling. Here we present the isolation and ovarian expression of the BmCAP gene which encodes CAP/ArgBP2 Gene /vinexin family proteins Gene in the silkmoth, Bombyx mori. Screening for full-length cDNA clones identified three mRNA isoforms, BmCAP-A1 Gene , BmCAP Gene - A2 Gene and BmCAP Gene -B, which show expression throughout ovarian follicular development. Using an antibody raised against a unique region between the SoHo and SH3 Gene domains, BmCAP-A Gene protein isoforms were identified that show specific expression in different compartments of the ovarian follicles. Immunofluorescence staining of the cells of the follicular epithelium establishes a dynamic pattern of BmCAP-A Gene protein localization during choriogenesis. During early choriogenesis, BmCAP Gene -A has a diffuse localization in the cytoplasm but could also be found concentrated at the apical and basal sides at the cell-cell junctions. During late choriogenesis, the diffuse cytoplasmic staining of BmCAP-A Gene disappears while the staining pattern at the apical side resembles a blueprint for the eggshell surface structure. We suggest that BmCAP Gene -A isoforms have important functions during ovarian development, which involve not only the traditional roles in actin organization or cell-cell adhesion but also the regulation of secretion of chorion proteins Gene and the sculpting of the chorion surface.
