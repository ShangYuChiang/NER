Molecular characterization of heat shock protein 90 Gene , 70 and 70 cognate cDNAs and their expression patterns during thermal stress and pupal diapause in the corn earworm. Three heat shock protein Gene transcripts, hsp90 Gene , hsp70 Gene , hsc70 Gene , isolated from the corn earworm, Helicoverpa zea, were evaluated for their responsiveness to diapause and thermal stress. These Hsps showed high homology to their counterparts in other species. A phylogenetic analysis of the Hsp90 Gene sequence was consistent with the known classification of insects. Northern blot hybridization indicated the presence of hsp90 Gene transcripts in all tissues, but expression in the brain-subesophageal complex was especially pronounced. The genomic organization of hsp90 Gene examined by Southern blot suggested the presence of a single copy of hsp90 Gene in the H. zea genome. The expression patterns after heat shock indicated that hsp70 Gene and hsp90 Gene were heat-inducible, although hsp70 Gene was more strongly induced than hsp90 Gene , and hsc70 Gene was indeed a constitutively expressed member of the hsp70 Gene family. Expression of hsp70 Gene and hsc70 Gene were not altered by the diapause program, but hsp90 Gene was down-regulated at this time. Low temperatures (0-4 degrees C) and recovery from low temperature elicited hsp70 Gene and hsp90 Gene responses, but not an hsc70 Gene response. Thus, unlike several other species, H. zea does not up-regulate hsp70 Gene during pupal diapause, but the down-regulation of hsp90 Gene is consistent with the pattern observed in several other species during diapause. Our results also indicate that hsp90 Gene and hsp70 Gene are responsive to low temperature in both diapausing and nondiapausing pupae.
