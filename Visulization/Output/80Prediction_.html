All-trans retinoic acid affects subcellular localization of a novel BmNIF3l Gene protein: functional deduce and tissue distribution of NIF3l Gene gene from silkworm (Bombyx mori). A novel cDNA sequence encoding a predicted protein of 271 amino acids containing a conserved NIF3 Gene domain was found from a pupal cDNA library of silkworm. The corresponding gene was named BmNIF3l Gene (Bombyx mori NGG1p Gene interacting factor 3-like Gene ). It was found by bioinformatics that BmNIF3l Gene gene consisted of five exons and four introns and BmNIF3l Gene had a high degree of homology to other NIF3-like Gene proteins, especially in the N-terminal and C-terminal regions. A His-tagged BmNIF3l Gene fusion protein with a molecular weight of approximately 33.6 kDa was expressed and purified to homogeneity. We have used the purified fusion protein to produce polyclonal antibodies against BmNIF3l Gene for histochemical analysis. Subcellular localization revealed that BmNIF3l Gene is a cytoplasmic protein that responds to all-trans retinoic acid (ATRA). Western blotting and real-time reverse transcription polymerase chain reaction showed that the expression level of BmNIF3l Gene is higher in tissues undergoing differentiation. Taken together, the results suggest that BmNIF3l Gene functions in transcription.
