Molecular cloning, genomic structure, and genetic mapping of two Rdl-orthologous genes of GABA receptors Gene in the diamondback moth, Plutella xylostella. The Resistance to dieldrin (Rdl) gene encodes a subunit of the insect gamma-aminobutyric acid ( GABA Gene ) receptor. Cyclodiene resistance in many insects is associated with replacement of a single amino acid (alanine at position 302) with either a serine or a glycine in the Rdl gene. Two Rdl-orthologous genes of GABA receptors ( PxGABARalpha1 Gene and PxGABARalpha2 Gene ) were cloned and sequenced from a susceptible strain (Roth) of Plutella xylostella. PxGABARalpha1 Gene and PxGABARalpha2 Gene showed 84% and 77% identity with the Rdl gene of Drosophila melanogaster at an amino acid level, respectively. The coding regions of PxGABARalpha1 Gene and PxGABARalpha2 Gene both comprise ten exons, with two alternative RNA-splicing forms in exon 3 of both genes. At the orthologous position of alanine-302 Gene in D. melanogaster Rdl, PxGABARalpha1 Gene has a conserved alanine at position 282. PxGABARalpha2 Gene has a serine instead of an alanine at the equivalent position. With two informative DNA markers, both PxGABARalpha1 Gene and PxGABARalpha2 Gene were mapped onto the Z chromosome of P. xylostella.
