Latrophilin Gene mediates insecticides susceptibility and fecundity through two carboxylesterases, esterase4 Gene and esterase6 Gene , in Tribolium castaneum. Latrophilin Gene ( LPH Gene ) is known as an adhesion G-protein-coupled receptor Gene which involved in multiple physiological processes in organisms. Previous studies showed that lph Gene not only involved the susceptibility to anticholinesterase insecticides but also affected fecundity in Tribolium castaneum. However, its regulatory mechanisms in these biological processes are still not clear. Here, we identified two potential downstream carboxylesterase Gene ( cce Gene ) genes of Tclph Gene , esterase4 Gene and esterase6 Gene , and further characterized their interactions with Tclph Gene . After treatment of T. castaneum larvae with carbofuran or dichlorvos insecticides, the transcript levels of Tcest4 Gene and Tcest6 Gene were significantly induced from 12 to 72 h. RNAi against Tcest4 Gene or Tcest6 Gene led to the higher mortality compared with the controls after the insecticides treatment, suggesting that these two genes play a vital role in detoxification of insecticides in T. castaneum. Furthermore, with insecticides exposure to Tclph Gene knockdown beetles, the expression of Tcest4 Gene was upregulated but Tcest6 Gene was downregulated, indicating that beetles existed a compensatory response against the insecticides. Additionally, RNAi of Tcest6 Gene resulted in 43% reductions in female egg laying and completely inhibited egg hatching, which showed the similar phenotype as that of Tclph Gene knockdown. These results indicated that Tclph Gene affected fecundity by positively regulating Tcest6 Gene expression. Our findings will provide a new insight into the molecular mechanisms of Tclph Gene involved in physiological functions in T. castaneum.
