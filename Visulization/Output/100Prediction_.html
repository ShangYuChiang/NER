Heat shock proteins Gene contribute to mosquito dehydration tolerance. This study examines the responses of heat shock protein Gene transcripts, Hsp70 Gene and Hsp90 Gene , to dehydration stress in three mosquito species, Aedes aegypti, Anopheles gambiae and Culex pipiens. We first defined the water balance attributes of adult females of each species, monitored expression of the hsp transcripts in response to dehydration, and then knocked down expression of the transcripts using RNA interference (RNAi) to evaluate potential functions of the Hsps in maintenance of water balance. Fully hydrated females of all three species contained nearly the same amount of water (66-68%), but water loss rates differed among the species, with A. aegypti having the lowest water loss rate ( 2.6%/h Gene ), followed by C. pipiens ( 3.3%/h Gene ), and A. gambiae ( 5.1%/h Gene ). In all three species water could be replaced only by drinking water (or blood). Both A. aegypti and C. pipiens tolerated a loss of 36% of their body water, but A. gambiae was more vulnerable to water loss, tolerating a loss of only 29% of its body water. Dehydration elicited expression of hsp70 Gene in all three species, but only C. pipiens continued to express this transcript during rehydration. Hsp90 Gene was constitutively expressed and expression levels remained fairly constant during dehydration and rehydration, except expression was not noted during rehydration of C. pipiens. Injection of dsRNA to knock down expression of hsp70 Gene (83% reduction) and hsp90 Gene (46% reduction) in A. aegypti did not alter water content or water loss rates, but the dehydration tolerance was lower. Instead of surviving a 36% water loss, females were able to survive only a 28% water loss in response to RNAi directed against hsp70 Gene and a 26% water loss when RNAi was directed against hsp90 Gene . These results indicate a critical function for these Hsps in mosquito dehydration tolerance.
