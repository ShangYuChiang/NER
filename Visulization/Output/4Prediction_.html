Influence of Metarhizium anisopliae ( IMI330189 Gene ) and Mad1 Gene protein on enzymatic activities and Toll-related genes of migratory locust. Efficacy of Metarhizium anisopliae strain ( IMI330189 Gene ) and Mad1 Gene protein alone or in combination by feeding method to overcome immune-related enzymes and Toll-like pathway genes was investigated in migratory locust. M. anisopliae ( IMI330189 Gene ) is a potent and entomopathogenic fungal strain could be effectively used against insect pests. Similarly, Mad1 Gene protein adheres to insect cuticle, causing virulence to insects. We confirmed maximum 55% of mortality when M. anisopliae ( IMI330189 Gene ) and Mad1 Gene was applied in combination. Similarly, increased PO activity was observed in locust with combined dose of Mad1 Gene + IMI330189 Gene whereas PO, POD, and SOD activities reduced using Mad1 Gene independently. Four Toll-like signaling pathway genes ( MyD88 Gene , Cactus Gene , Pelle Gene , and CaN Gene ) were investigated from midgut and body of the migratory locust after 72 h of treatments. Subsequently, the expression of MyD88 Gene in the midgut and body significantly decreased with the application of Mad1 Gene and Mad1 Gene + IMI330189 Gene . Performance of these treatments was absolutely non-consistent in both parts of insects. Meanwhile, IMI330189 Gene significantly raised the expression of Cactus Gene in both midgut and body. However, the combined treatment ( Mad1 Gene + IMI330189 Gene ) significantly reduced the Cactus Gene expression in both body parts. Pelle Gene expression was significantly increased in the midgut with the application of independent treatment of Mad1 Gene and IMI330189 Gene whereas the combined treatment ( Mad1 Gene + IMI330189 Gene ) suppressed the Pelle Gene expression in midgut. Its expression level was absolutely higher in body with the application of IMI330189 Gene and Mad1 Gene + IMI330189 Gene only. On the other hand, Mad1 Gene significantly increased the expression of CaN in midgut. However, all three treatments significantly affected and suppressed the expression of CaN gene in body of locust. This shows that the applications of M. anisopliae and Mad1 Gene protein significantly affected Toll signaling pathway genes, which ultimately increased level of susceptibility of locust. However, their effect was significantly different in both parts of locust which recommends that the Toll-related genes are conserved in midgut instead of locust body.
