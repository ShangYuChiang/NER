Identification and Expression Analysis of Four Small Heat Shock Protein Gene Genes in Cigarette Beetle, Lasioderma serricorne (Fabricius). Small heat shock proteins ( sHsps Gene ) are molecular chaperones that play crucial roles in the stress adaption of insects. In this study, we identified and characterized four sHsp Gene genes ( LsHsp19.4 Gene , 20.2, 20.3, and 22.2) from the cigarette beetle, Lasioderma serricorne (Fabricius). The four cDNAs encoded proteins of 169, 180, 181, and 194 amino acids with molecular weights of 19.4, 20.2, 20.3, and 22.2 kDa, respectively. The four LsHsp Gene sequences possessed a typical sHsp Gene domain structure. Quantitative real-time PCR analyses revealed that LsHsp19.4 Gene and 20.3 transcripts were most abundant in pupae, whereas the transcript levels of LsHsp20.2 Gene and 22.2 were highest in adults. Transcripts of three LsHsp Gene genes were highly expressed in the larval fat body, whereas LsHsp20.2 Gene displayed an extremely high expression level in the gut. Expression of the four LsHsp Gene genes was dramatically upregulated in larvae exposed to 20-hydroxyecdysone Gene . The majority of the LsHsp Gene genes were significantly upregulated in response to heat and cold treatments, while LsHsp19.4 Gene was insensitive to cold stress. The four genes were upregulated when challenged by immune triggers (peptidoglycan isolated from Staphylococcus aureus and from Escherichia coli 0111: B4 Gene ). Exposure to CO2 Gene increased LsHsp20.2 Gene and 20.3 transcript levels, but the LsHsp19.4 Gene transcript level declined. The results suggest that different LsHsp Gene genes play important and distinct regulatory roles in L. serricorne development and in response to diverse stresses.
