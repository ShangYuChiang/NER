Evolutionary divergence of the paralogs Methoprene tolerant Gene ( Met Gene ) and germ cell expressed Gene ( gce Gene ) within the genus Drosophila. Juvenile hormone (JH) signaling underpins both regulatory and developmental pathways in insects. However, the JH receptor Gene is poorly understood. Methoprene tolerant Gene ( Met Gene ) and germ cell expressed Gene ( gce Gene ) have been implicated in JH signaling in Drosophila. We investigated the evolution of Met Gene and gce Gene across 12 Drosophila species and found that these paralogs are conserved across at least 63 million years of dipteran evolution. Distinct patterns of selection found using estimates of dN/dS ratios across Drosophila Met Gene and gce Gene coding sequences, along with their incongruent temporal expression profiles in embryonic Drosophila melanogaster, illustrate avenues through which these genes have diverged within the Diptera. Additionally, we demonstrate that the annotated gene CG15032 is the 5 terminus of gce Gene . In mosquitoes and beetles, a single Met Gene -like homolog displays structural similarity to both Met Gene and gce Gene , and the intron locations are conserved with those of gce Gene . We found that Tribolium and mosquito Met Gene orthologs are assembled from Met- Gene and gce Gene -specific domains in a modular fashion. Our results suggest that Drosophila Met Gene and gce Gene experienced divergent evolutionary pressures following the duplication of an ancestral gce Gene -like gene found in less derived holometabolous insects.
