Cloning and characterization of a 70 kDa heat shock cognate Gene gene ( HSC70 Gene ) from two species of Chironomus. In the present study we carried out the isolation and characterization of an HSC70 Gene gene from two midges, Chironomus tentans and C. yoshimatsui. The HSC70 Gene cDNAs are approximately 2424 (C. tentans) and 2464 bp (C. yoshimatsui) long, and contain 1950 and 1956 bp open reading frames, respectively. Analysis of genomic DNA revealed the presence of two introns in these genes. The 5 untranslated regions of the HSC70 Gene genes are adenosine-rich, a feature found in inducible HSP70 Gene genes. The nucleotide and amino acid sequences exhibit high identity with cytosolic HSC70s from other Dipterans. Northern hybridization indicated that HSC70 Gene is expressed at all developmental stages, from embryo to adult, and Southern hybridization confirmed the presence of multiple HSP70 Gene genes in Chironomus.
