Identification and functional characterization of an odorant receptor Gene in pea aphid, Acyrthosiphon pisum. The sensitive olfactory system is necessary for survival of insects. Odorant receptors Gene ( ORs Gene ) are located on the dendrites of olfactory receptor neurons and play a critical role in odor detection. Insect ORs Gene are functionally analyzed via heterologous expression in a Xenopus oocyte system using a two-electrode voltage-clamp (TEVC) electrophysiological recording. Here, we have identified a novel OR Gene in the pea aphid, Acyrthosiphon pisum, then we cloned and named it ApisOR4 Gene . We analyzed the ApisOR4 Gene tissue expression patterns and found expression only in antennae tissues. Further functional analysis using TEVC revealed that ApisOR4 Gene is broadly tuned to eight volatiles, which elicit electrophysiological response in pea aphid antennae. This study provides an initial functional analysis of aphid ORs Gene and identifies candidate volatiles to be used in developing new strategies for aphid control.
